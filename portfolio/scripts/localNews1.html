Florida lawmakers unanimously pass bill to combat squatting

March 8, 2024 - State lawmakers have unanimously passed a bill to combat squatting.

This comes after an investigation in jacksonville,done by News4JAX, exposing how a loophole in the law allowed people to live rent-free in a home for weeks while the property's owner wasn't allowed on the premises. The bill would close that loophole, and it's now headed to the governor's desk.

The bill allows law enforcement to immediately remove squatters who do not have a lease authorized by the property owner. It also adds criminal penalties, including designating intentionally presenting a fraudulent lease as a misdemeanor and making it a felony to intentionally sell or lease someone else's property or intentionally cause more than $1,000 in property damage while squatting or trespassing.
